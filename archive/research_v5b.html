Research

My research can broadly be classified as pertaining to oceanic mesoscale and submesoscale turbulence. By these terms, we refer to different dynamical regimes. For instance, the term "mesoscale" refers to ocean features characterised by horizontal scales of tens to hundreds of kilometers and temporal scales of days to months. These are the typical signals seen from space, for instance, from active microwave altimeters and passive microwave radiometers measurements. They correspond to nearly two-dimensional flows in which the dominant forces acting on a fluid parcel are those due to Earth's rotation and pressure gradient forces.

In contrast, the "submesoscale" regime corresponds to ocean features characterised by horizontal scales of hundreds of meters to tens of kilometers and temporal scales of days to months. At these scales, other forces in the equations of motion become important.
